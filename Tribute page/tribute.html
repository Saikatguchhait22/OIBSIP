Tribute to Rabindranath Thakur

Introduction

Rabindranath Tagore was an Indian poet, writer, playwright, composer, philosopher, social reformer and painter. He reshaped Bengali literature and music as well as Indian art with Contextual Modernism in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Author of the "profoundly sensitive, fresh and beautiful" poetry of Gitanjali, he became in 1913 the first non-European and the first lyricist to win the Nobel Prize in Literature.Tagore's poetic songs were viewed as spiritual and mercurial; where his elegant prose and magical poetry were widely popular in the Indian subcontinent. He was a fellow of the Royal Asiatic Society. Referred to as "the Bard of Bengal", Tagore was known by sobriquets: Gurudeb, Kobiguru, and Biswokobi.

Major Achievements

  • Rabindranath reshaped Bengali Literature.
  • He was the first non-European and lyricist to win Nobel Prize.
  • Was a fellow of the Royal Asiatic Society.
  • Started writing at the age of 8.

Legacy

Rabindranath Tagore, a renowned literary hero, is one of the world most influential creatives. He brewed a legacy between the 19th and 20th century which continues to live on. Some of his most famous works include Gitanjali (1910), Kabuliwala (1961) and The Postmaster (1918) to name a few. Rabindranath Tagore also impacted the political scene of India with his poetry. The themes in his work continued to be remembered for its influence in literature and culture. We explore the rich legacy of South Asia most famous poet, philosopher and scholar Rabindranath Tagore.
